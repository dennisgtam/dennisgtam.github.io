Technical Blog 3: Arrays and Hashes

July 27th, 2015

This week I'm going to talk about two very important and different data structures: arrays and hashes. Let's dive right into it.

Arrays

An array is a data structure that can hold multiple values. Unlike other languages, Ruby arrays can be of any size. They can also contain multiple types of objects. An array can contain strings, integers, arrays, and hashes.

Arrays are like ordered lists. You can access the elements of the list with an index. The index starts at zero. Thus, to access the first element of the list we call array_name[0]. The second element would be accessed with array_name[1]. You can also access the last element with array_name[-1], the second to last element with array_name[-2] etc.

Cultural Blog 1

July 11th, 2015

Alright, here's the first cultural blog for DevBootcamp. I want to talk about DBC learning culture, my experience so far, and impostor syndrome.

In the video, Shereef made a comment about the two most common approaches people have with DBC: a consumer mindset and an active mindset. A consumer will go into the program expecting things to be handed to them, that DBC promises that you'll learn these things and that DBC will teach you them.

On the other hand, an active mindset comes into DBC seeing oppportunity and the chance to reach out and expand beyond what might just be said in lectures. So far, I feel like the curriculum encourages an active style of learning. The instructions are clear but there's a lot of research, experimentation, and self study that I needed to do to really feel comfortable about this stuff. I like this style of teaching a lot and there's a great community of people that I can reach out to if I need help.
