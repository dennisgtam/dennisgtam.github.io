Cultural Blog 5: Feedback

August 10, 2015

I'm wrapping up week 5 of phase 0 of Devbootcamp and it's time to reflect on some of the learning processes, namely feedback.

It's been interesting pairing with fellow students to work over challenges. I enjoy it because I get to learn a lot about how other people approach problems. I've also learned a lot of new methods from other people's code. It feels very rewarding to teach another person your approach and to gain something from them as well.

While nothing has been extremely difficult for me just yet. Pairing has pointed out some of my own weaknesess. I tend to dominate the conversation some times and my pair partner has difficulty getting a word in. It's not something I do intentionally and it's something I'll have to work on.

The feedback I've received has been pretty good so far. They've been constructive and kind. I really learned a lot about myself from my pairs feedback. As I mentioned earlier, it really has helped me determine my weaknesses and given me a chance to fix them. On the other hand, writing feedback has been a little difficult for me. I haven't found too many actionable tings to say since I feel most pairing sessions go well. All in all, I think it's been a good time so far.
